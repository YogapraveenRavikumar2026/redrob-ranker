The Core Problem

Why Keyword Matching Fails

Traditional candidate search systems rely on keyword matching — and they're fundamentally broken.

🔍

Semantic Blindness

Keyword filters miss Tier-5 candidates who built RAG before it had a name. They can't understand synonyms or context.

👻

Ghost Candidates

A perfect skill list means nothing if the candidate hasn't logged in for 6 months. Traditional systems ignore activity signals entirely.

🎭

Gaming the System

HR Managers with 9 AI keywords rank above ML Engineers who shipped real production systems. Keyword stuffing goes undetected.

⚠️ The sample submission proves this — rank 1 is an HR Manager
Our Approach

What Great Recruiters Actually Do

❌ Keyword Matching
  • Matches literal words, ignoring synonyms
  • Treats all skills equally regardless of depth
  • Ignores availability and activity signals
  • Easily gamed by keyword stuffers
  • No fraud or honeypot detection
VS
✅ Recruiter Brain
  • Reads career trajectory and progression
  • Weighs production experience vs. theory
  • Checks availability, response rate, and notice
  • Detects and filters impossible profiles
  • Generates fact-grounded reasoning

Scoring Engine

5-Axis Composite Scoring

Not just keywords — we evaluate candidates across five distinct dimensions.

Semantic Fit Cosine similarity between JD & resume embeddings
35%
Core Skills Python, ML production, vector DBs, ranking systems
30%
Career Trajectory Job stability, product company tenure, role progression
15%
Availability Active last 90d, open to work, response rate, notice period
15%
Location Fit India-based, Tier-1 city matching, relocation willingness
5%

🛡️ Honeypot Defense System

🚨 Expert skill explosion (8+ skills, <5 yrs exp)
🎭 Title-skill mismatch (HR title + deep AI skills)
📅 Date inconsistency (end date before start date)
⏱️ Career duration overflow (impossible timelines)
👻 Ghost profile (95% complete, zero activity)
Flagged profiles receive a ×0.05 penalty — effectively eliminated.
